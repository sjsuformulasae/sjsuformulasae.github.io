Powertrain

The Powertrain sub-team is responsible for the design, manufacture, and testing of all the engine related systems on the vehicle. These systems include the engine and transmission, differential and final drive, cooling , electrical, intake, pneumatic shifting, and the calibration of these systems. Some of the disciplines covered through the design process are CAD/CAM, engine simulation, Intake/exhaust CFD, circuit design, drivetrain simulation, and extensive engine and drivetrain tuning.

The purpose of the exhaust system is to remove the waste gas out of the engine through a series of pipes. For performance in a naturally aspirated engine, exhaust must be tuned with the intake system to scavenge gases at a certain point in the RPM range in order to pull in more fresh air, increasing the power output. An issue we encountered was welding the exhaust. Exhaust material used was titanium. Another issue was midpipe placement. The chassis had a design change that we had to account for, so midpipe design had to change. With this chassis change, muffler mounting points also change.

EMAIL: andrewgonzalez181@gmail.com

YEAR: Senior

The system that I co-designed was the front wing. The front wing has the task of manipulating the incoming air to generate a force towards the ground. It works in conjunction with other aerodynamic components, to push our vehicle firmly on the ground. This “push”allows our vehicle to grip the ground, increasing its traction and cornering speed capabilities. A particular challenge with the front wing is its relation to other aerodynamic devices. While the placement of front wing allows it to receive a clear stream of air, it has the potential to disrupt the air flow to other aerodynamic devices; negating their effects. Thus, the front wing has to be designed with consideration to the other aerodynamic components. This consideration assists us in designing an optimized, effective and competitive vehicle.

As a system designer, I oversaw the mounting structure that connected the front wing to the chassis and I was a co-designer for the front wing. The purpose of the front wing is to create a downforce that will allow the tires to transmit a greater thrust force that will maximize the vehicle’s acceleration and to direct the vehicle’s airflow. The front wing itself governs the overall aerodynamics of the car, while the goal of the mount is to withstand the aerodynamic force of the downforce and drag generated by the front wing. How To Join!

Welcome to the SJSU Formula SAE Team; Spartan Racing
Before diving head first into the world of FSAE, you will need to become an official Formula SAE member. This document will go over all of the necessary steps required to join the Spartan Racing Internal Combustion team.

Steps:

In order to become an Intern on the Spartan Racing Combustion team, you must complete the steps below. Please note, there is no particular order you must complete these steps in.

  1. Choose a Sub-Team to join for the Season
    • Spartan Racing is comprised of five different Sub-Teams (Aerodynamics, Chassis, Powertrain, Suspension and Business). While it is not required, we do recommend that new students attend a variety of Sub-Team meetings before making their final decision.
  2. Pay your $40 team fee
    • Engineering a new race car every year gets expensive. With everyone contributing a small monetary amount we can help offset our manufacturing and operating costs.
  3. Complete the Online Application
    • By having new members fill out this online application we gain valuable information about who is on our team. From these applications we can easily have access to your contact information and have a greater understanding of each individual’s skillsets and connections.

At the time of an intern you cannot be a major system designer, buy any team gear (except t shirts), mention FSAE on a resume; an intern is on a trial basis. At end of the semester, we will assess each intern and either make them a full member, continue as intern, or take away intern status and ask to apply during next fall when schedule/availability allows for FSAE.
